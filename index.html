1. A "Zero to One" Fundamental Leap
Bemp’s Li-S/B4C-Hemp architecture is a fundamental leap in physics and material science, akin to Nikola Tesla’s invention of alternating current (AC). We do not have "just a concept." We hold functional prototype data, independently verified by Dr. Deyang Qu and his expert team at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee (UWM).
To ensure absolute impartiality and no conflict of financial interests, UWM staff held zero equity in Bemp during the testing phase. Upon reviewing our prototype's data, Dr. Qu explicitly stated on the record: "No one ever got this far."
2. The Genesis: Cooked Off-Grid
A crucial distinction: We did not "cook" the batteries. We synthesized the core carbon matrix.
The carbon samples for Cell #1 (Pure Hemp) and Cell #4 (Hemp mixed with Boron via ultrasonic stirring) were cooked in a makeshift, off-grid warehouse. Powered entirely by two small gas generators, our small high-pressure furnace never exceeded 1000°C. These raw materials were then sent to UWM, who assembled them into the 2032 coin cells you see modeled below.
This proves the extreme, rugged scalability and low cost of our processes. If we can synthesize world-class battery materials in an off-grid shed, our future solar-powered gigafactories will completely obliterate legacy manufacturing costs.
3. The Architectures: Cell #1 vs Cell #4
Our supply chain is brutally simple, relying on an inherently low-cost Bill of Materials (C65 carbon black, pure lithium, hemp, sulfur). Below are the exact, verified specifications of the two foundational cells tested at UWM.
Cell #1 (The Commercializable Baseline)
Cathode
91% Bemp Material (70% Sulfur + 30% purely Carbonized Hemp), 4% C65 carbon black, 5% PVDF. Sulfur loading: ~3.5 mg/cm2.
Electrolyte
Ultra-Lean (E/S ≤ 3). 20 μL liquid (1M LiTFSI + 0.2M LiNO3 in DOL/DME). Volume is no more than triple the sulfur weight. Proves commercial mass-production viability.
Anode & Sep
Pure Lithium foil anode. Celgard 2325 separator. Aluminum foil current collector.
Cell #4 (The Kinetic Armor)
Cathode
85% B4C-Hemp Material (70% Sulfur + 30% Bemp with Boron Carbide), 10% C65, 5% PVDF. Sulfur loading: ~1.6 mg/cm2.
Electrolyte
Flooded (E/S ~ 12). 25 μL liquid (same salts/solvents). Designed specifically to accommodate heavy kinetic testing.
Anode & Sep
Pure Lithium foil (250 μm thickness). Celgard 2325 separator. 2032 Coin Cell format.
4. Power Density: Decrypting the 3C Metric
Cell #1 (The Baseline): Achieved a massive ~1150-1200 mAh/g specific capacity at 0.01C, successfully utilizing the ultra-lean E/S = 3. Even with the kinetic drop-off to ~750 mAh/g at 0.1C, this capacity absolutely crushes legacy Li-ion.
Cell #4 (The Kinetic Armor): Tested under continuous 3C charging (draining the entire battery in just 20 minutes).
A crucial physics note: Because the theoretical capacity of Lithium-Sulfur (1675 mAh/g) is vastly superior to standard Li-ion (~200 mAh/g), a 3C rate in Li-S unleashes an exponentially larger absolute power density. Cell #4 sustained ~450 mAh/g at 3C. In the battery world, maintaining this output over 100 cycles without structural collapse is sheer kinetic brutality. The Boron Carbide successfully locked down the polysulfide shuttle.

5. Bendability & The NahKah Sovereign Wheel
Legacy batteries use rigid, brittle metal oxides (like Cobalt and Nickel). Our architecture uses a matrix of Sulfur, porous Hemp carbon, and PVDF polymers. This chemical reality means our Li-S/B4C-Hemp cells are inherently bendable and durable.
This flexibility allows us to manufacture modular battery rings that literally wrap around the direct-drive hub motor of the NahKah Sovereign Wheel. By cross-pollinating Cell #1 and #4 into hybrid packs (Cell #7 & #8), we achieve a targeted pack density of ~428 Wh/kg.
