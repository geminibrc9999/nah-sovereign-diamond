Ultra-Lean Liquid Electrolyte (E/S = 3)
E/S Ratio Explained: The ratio of liquid volume to sulfur weight. Many lab experiments cheat by "flooding" cells with liquid (E/S > 10). Bemp achieved success at a commercially scalable E/S ratio of 3 or less (using 1M LiTFSI + 0.2M LiNO3), proving mass-production viability.
Anode: Pure Lithium Foil
Instead of heavy, dead-weight graphite, we use pure metallic lithium (30-250 μm thick), maximizing gravimetric energy density.
3. Cell #1: The Commercializable Baseline
Tested using the ultra-lean E/S = 3 protocol, our team achieved a massive ~1150-1200 mAh/g specific capacity at a slow 0.01C discharge rate. Proving functionality at this low liquid ratio is the ultimate proof that our chemistry can graduate from coin cells to commercially scalable pouch cells. Even with the standard kinetic drop-off to ~750 mAh/g at a 0.1C rate, this capacity absolutely crushes legacy Li-ion. Cell #1 in its current form is ready for static grid applications.
